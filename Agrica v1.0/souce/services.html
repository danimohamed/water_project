Water Reservoir Levels in Morocco

The infographic provides vital data on the water reservoir levels across various basins in Morocco, which is essential for efficient water resource management and planning. With a total volume of 5,150.64 million cubic meters stored in reservoirs and an overall fill rate of 31.95%, these statistics offer a snapshot of the current water availability in the region. The fill rates vary significantly among the basins, from as high as 62.90% in the Loukkos basin to as low as 6.75% in the Oum Er Rbia basin.

This variability in water levels is critical for multiple sectors including agriculture, industry, and municipal services, particularly in a country where water supply can be unpredictable and often insufficient. For instance, higher fill rates in regions like Loukkos and Tensift might allow for more robust agricultural activities or industrial usage, while lower rates in areas like Oum Er Rbia could signal potential water shortages requiring immediate conservation measures.

Furthermore, the color-coded map enhances understanding by visually distinguishing regions based on their water security, highlighting areas that might need urgent attention or could withstand a drier season. By monitoring these levels closely, stakeholders can make informed decisions to allocate resources, implement water-saving initiatives, and prepare for potential droughts, ensuring sustainable water management across Morocco.
